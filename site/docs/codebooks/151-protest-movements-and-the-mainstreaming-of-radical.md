## Description

Radical and extremist actors leverage social media to subtly embed their worldviews into everyday discourse. Drawing on mainstreaming theory, we examine whether protest movements can facilitate such processes by creating environments conducive to a public shift toward the extremes. We conceptualize protest-facilitated mainstreaming (PFM) as a process that operates through content and connective mechanisms on social media and explore the case of the German COVID-19-measures protest movement “Querdenken,” which sparked interest from sympathizers of various backgrounds. Using a multi-layered Telegram dataset in a quantitative-computational approach, we triangulate longitudinal content analysis, URL analysis, and network analysis.
